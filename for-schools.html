Why early years settings use Sugar Dragon

Children learn habits at nursery and carry them home

Tooth decay is the most common reason children aged 5 to 9 are admitted to hospital in England. Early years settings are where habits form. A five-minute read-aloud, a brushing chart on the wall, and a villain the children can defeat: that is the full Sugar Dragon classroom offer.

The books are written in AABB rhyming couplets for group read-aloud. The free classroom brushing chart includes a class tracker column so every child can record their daily wins.

No lesson plan needed. No assessment. No screen time required. The Sugar Dragon does the work.

EYFS alignment

  • ✓

    Physical Development: health and self-care

    Children understand the importance of healthy food choices and tooth brushing as part of a daily self-care routine. Sugar Dragon makes the behaviour explicit and motivating.

  • ✓

    Personal, Social and Emotional Development

    Children develop self-regulation by taking responsibility for defeating the Dragon. The streak chart builds delayed gratification and a sense of personal achievement.

  • ✓

    Communication and Language

    Rhyming picture books with a strong narrative arc build vocabulary, phonological awareness, and listening skills in the 6 months to 5 years age group.

  • ✓

    Clinically reviewed

    All brushing guidance in the books and classroom materials is reviewed by Mr S Ditta, registered UK dental hygienist, and follows NHS Delivering Better Oral Health (2021).
